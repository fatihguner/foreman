# Example 8: Combined Modes -- Solo + Stoic + Japanese

## Scenario

Yuki is a solo founder in Tokyo building KatachiLab, a browser-based design tool for freelance graphic designers in the Japanese market. Bootstrapped, $6,000 MRR from 140 paying users. Last month, her biggest client -- a design agency accounting for 30% of her revenue -- cancelled their subscription and moved to a competing platform. Revenue dropped to $4,200 MRR overnight. Yuki is demoralised. She built the product, manages support, handles billing, writes the docs, and now faces a strategic crisis alone. Her message: "Ichiban ookii kokyaku wo ushinaimashita. Senryaku wo kangaenaoshitai desu."

**Modes activated:** `/solo` + `/stoic on` + `/language japanese`

**Purpose of this example:** Demonstrate how three modes layer simultaneously without conflict. Solo mode filters WHAT content is relevant. Stoic mode frames HOW that content is received. Language mode determines IN WHICH LANGUAGE everything is delivered. Show one complete skill output with all three modes visible.

## Behind the Scenes: How Three Modes Layer

### Mode Interaction Summary

**Solo (WHAT):** Filters content -- removes team references, remaps audience to self, replaces Owner with Method column, adds personal capacity framing.

**Stoic (HOW):** Adds depth -- Seven Functions diagnostic, Reverse Clause on weaknesses, virtue framing at decisions, agency closing on action plans.

**Language (IN WHICH LANGUAGE):** Transforms output -- all text in Japanese, framework names bilingual on first mention, desu/masu register, business keigo.

### One Output, Three Visible Layers

The SWOT Weaknesses table demonstrates all three modes in a single element. **Solo** contributed the "solo context" column (weaknesses as one-person constraints, not team issues). **Stoic** contributed the "Reverse Clause" column (each weakness paired with what it makes possible -- a column that does not exist in normal mode). **Language** rendered the entire table in natural business Japanese.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Three modes occupy orthogonal dimensions.** Solo filters content, Stoic frames presentation, Language determines tongue. They cannot conflict because they never compete for the same axis. The SWOT weaknesses table proves it: Solo shaped the columns (personal, not organisational), Stoic added the Reverse Clause column (does not exist in normal mode), Language rendered it all in Japanese.

2. **The combination is more than additive.** No single mode could produce what Yuki received: a SWOT that evaluates personal capabilities, pairs every weakness with its opportunity, separates controllable from uncontrollable, and delivers it in natural business Japanese.

3. **Stoic mode is particularly potent for solo founders.** No team to share emotional weight, no board for accountability, no co-founder for reality checks. The Seven Functions diagnostic gave Yuki a structured self-examination. The Amor Fati assessment named her emotional position without judgment and provided a path from Resistance to Acceptance -- a self-directed alternative to feedback loops team-based founders take for granted.

4. **Language mode preserves analytical precision.** The Japanese output is strategic analysis conducted in English and expressed in Japanese -- not translation. Sentence structure follows Japanese conventions, business vocabulary uses established terms, and desu/masu register maintains institutional authority.

5. **Commands stay English; everything else switches.** `/solo`, `/stoic on`, `/language japanese` in English. Every subsequent word in Japanese. Internal routing, skill selection, and diagnostic logic remained in English, ensuring the language switch never affected analytical accuracy.
