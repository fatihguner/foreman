## Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Sustained non-engagement** | Three cancelled or avoided meetings | Engage through trusted intermediary or escalate to superior |
| **Active sabotage** | Covert lobbying, resource withholding, misinformation | Document, present evidence to decision-maker, strengthen coalition |
| **Immovable veto** | Veto holder refuses engagement after two direct attempts | Redesign initiative to remove veto dependency, or escalate to override authority |
| **Opposition coalition** | Resister recruits others into active opposition | Shift to coalition-vs-coalition strategy via coalition-and-partnership-games |
| **Values impasse** | Three conversations confirm irreconcilable conflict | Accept impasse; pursue compromise, restructuring, or initiative redesign |

---

## De-escalation Techniques

| Technique | When to Use | Execution |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **Acknowledge and validate** | Resister feels unheard | Name their concern as legitimate before proposing alternatives |
| **Separate person from position** | Conflict has become personal | Reframe around shared interests and organizational outcomes |
| **Concede the peripheral** | Both substantive and emotional objections | Yield on a visible but non-critical point to demonstrate good faith |
| **Introduce neutral mediator** | Direct engagement turned adversarial | Trusted third party facilitates the conversation |
| **Cooling period** | High emotions after confrontation | Pause 48-72 hours, re-engage through lower-temperature channel |
