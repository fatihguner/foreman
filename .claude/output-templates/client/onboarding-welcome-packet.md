## Getting Started

_Complete these steps in order. Each builds on the previous one._

1. **Confirm your kickoff meeting.** Check your calendar for the invitation sent to [email/calendar system]. If you do not see it within 24 hours of receiving this packet, contact [Project Lead name] at [email]. Attendees should include: [list recommended roles -- e.g., "your project sponsor, day-to-day point of contact, and technical lead"].

2. **Designate your internal champions.** Identify one to two people on your team who will serve as primary users and internal advocates during onboarding. Share their names and contact details with [Project Lead name] so we can tailor training to their workflows.

3. **Provide system access.** We will send a secure access request form within 48 hours of contract execution. Complete it promptly -- access provisioning is the most common cause of onboarding delays.

4. **Review the scope document.** Revisit the agreed scope of work attached to your contract. Flag any questions or clarifications before the kickoff meeting so we can address them immediately rather than carrying ambiguity into the project.

5. **Set up your communication channels.** Join [communication platform -- e.g., "the dedicated Slack channel," "the shared project workspace," "the client portal at [URL]"]. This is where day-to-day communication will happen between scheduled meetings.

6. **Prepare your onboarding team.** Block calendar time for the training sessions outlined in the first-30-days timeline above. Attendance in the first two weeks directly correlates with adoption speed -- teams that skip onboarding sessions consistently take 2-3x longer to reach proficiency.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What if we need to change the scope after we start?**

Scope changes happen. We handle them through a documented change request process: you describe what you need, we assess impact on timeline and cost, and both parties agree in writing before work begins. Minor adjustments within the spirit of the original scope are absorbed without formality.

**2. What happens if something goes wrong?**

Contact your Account Manager directly. If the issue is technical and urgent, use the support channel listed in the Key Contacts section. For unresolved concerns, the escalation path runs from Project Lead to Account Manager to Executive Sponsor. We would rather hear about a problem early than discover it late.

**3. How long does onboarding typically take?**

Most clients are operating independently on core workflows within 30 days. Complex implementations with significant integrations or large user bases may extend to 45-60 days. Your Project Lead will provide a tailored timeline during kickoff.

**4. Can we add more users or services later?**

Yes. Expansion is straightforward -- contact your Account Manager to discuss additional capacity, and we will provide a proposal within [X] business days.

**5. What if we are not satisfied with the service?**

Raise it immediately. The quarterly business review is the formal checkpoint, but feedback is welcome at any time. Our goal is to address concerns before they become grievances. The feedback channel below exists precisely for this purpose.
