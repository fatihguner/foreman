## 1. Problem Statement

<!-- The proposal lives or dies here. If the reader does not recognize their own problem
     in your words, they will read no further. Write from THEIR perspective, not yours. -->

[Two to three paragraphs. Describe the challenge, opportunity, or gap the client faces. Demonstrate that you understand the problem at a level equal to or deeper than the client's own understanding. Cite specific conditions, market forces, or operational realities that make this problem urgent now rather than theoretical. Do not mention your solution yet -- earn the right to propose it by proving you grasp what needs solving.]

**The cost of inaction:** [One to two sentences quantifying or qualifying what happens if the client does nothing. Make the status quo uncomfortable.]

## 2. Proposed Solution

**In one sentence:** [What you propose to do. Clear enough to repeat from memory.]

**Solution architecture:**

[Three to five paragraphs describing the solution. Structure this as a narrative, not a feature list. Explain what you will deliver, how it addresses the problem described in Section 1, and what makes this approach distinct from alternatives the client may be considering. Connect each element of the solution back to a specific dimension of the problem.]
