# Compensation Review Brief

Compensation decisions are retention bets disguised as expense line items. Underpaying a critical executive saves money quarterly and costs millions at departure. Overpaying a mediocre one compounds in both cash and cultural signal. This brief provides the data for deliberate decisions. Replace all placeholders and delete this paragraph before distribution.

## Compensation Philosophy

[Company Name] targets [Xth percentile — e.g., 50th, 75th] of base compensation and [Xth percentile] of total compensation (including equity) relative to [peer set definition — e.g., venture-backed SaaS companies, Series A-B, 50-200 employees, primary metro areas]. Equity is weighted toward early employees and roles with direct impact on enterprise value. Cash compensation reflects market rates sufficient to eliminate compensation as a reason to leave, without attempting to compete on cash alone with public companies or late-stage peers.
