# Decision Rationale Memo

**MEMORANDUM**

**To:** [Investor Name(s) / Board]
**From:** [Your Name], [Title], [Company Name]
**Date:** [Date]
**Re:** [Decision Title -- e.g., "Decision to Enter the European Market" / "Pricing Model Transition from Per-Seat to Usage-Based" / "Strategic Partnership with [Company X]"]

## The Decision

[Company Name] has decided to [state the decision in plain, specific language]. This decision takes effect [date or trigger condition] and commits the company to [describe the primary resource commitment -- capital, headcount, timeline, or opportunity cost]. This memo presents the analysis, alternatives considered, risks acknowledged, and conditions under which we would reverse course.

## The Problem This Decision Addresses

<!-- Define the problem with precision. A well-defined problem contains the seeds of its solution. -->

[What changed -- internally or externally -- that made the status quo untenable? Ground this in data: a metric that moved, a market shift, a competitive action, a customer signal.]

[Consequences of inaction. What happens if the company does nothing? Quantify where possible.]

[The core tension. Name the trade-off explicitly. E.g., "Expanding into Europe accelerates revenue but divides engineering focus during product maturation."]

## How We Explored the Problem

[Analytical methods applied: scenario planning, decision matrices, customer research, financial modeling. Name inputs and sources.]

## Alternatives Considered

<!-- Present each alternative fairly -- as if advocating for it -- before explaining why it was rejected. -->

### Alternative A: [Name or brief description]

[One paragraph describing this alternative as its strongest advocate would present it. What are its merits? What problem does it solve?]

**Why rejected:** [One paragraph. Specific reasons -- data, feasibility constraints, risk profile, or misalignment with strategic priorities. Not "it was not as good" -- state precisely why.]

### Alternative B: [Name or brief description]

[One paragraph describing this alternative at its strongest.]

**Why rejected:** [One paragraph with specific reasoning.]

### Alternative C: [Status Quo / Do Nothing]

[One paragraph describing the case for inaction. There is always a case for inaction, and presenting it honestly strengthens the argument for action.]

**Why rejected:** [One paragraph. Typically grounded in the cost of delay, competitive dynamics, or a closing window of opportunity.]

## The Chosen Path: Detailed Rationale

[Paragraph 1: The primary reason. The single most important factor, supported by the strongest evidence.]

## Decision Traps Audit

We audited this decision process against common cognitive traps:

- **Anchoring:** [Describe how you tested whether the first data point or option unduly influenced the analysis. E.g., "Initial financial projections anchored on [source]. We reran the model using [alternative source] as a base case to test sensitivity."]
- **Sunk cost:** [Describe how you ensured that prior investment in the status quo did not bias toward inaction or toward doubling down. E.g., "The $X already invested in [prior approach] was explicitly excluded from the go-forward analysis."]
- **Confirmation bias:** [Describe what disconfirming evidence you actively sought. E.g., "We conducted five customer interviews specifically designed to surface objections to the proposed approach."]
- **Overconfidence:** [Describe how you calibrated confidence in projections. E.g., "All financial projections include a 30% downside sensitivity. The decision remains positive under all scenarios above [threshold]."]
