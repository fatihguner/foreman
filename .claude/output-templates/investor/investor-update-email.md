# Investor Update Email

An investor update is not a report — it is a relationship maintenance instrument. The updates that unlock introductions, follow-on capital, and crisis counsel share three properties: brevity, honesty, and a specific ask. Fill all placeholders, strip all comments, send within the first week of the period. Silence erodes trust faster than bad news.

---

**Subject:** [Company Name] — [Month / Q#] [Year] Update

Hi [First Name / "team"],

[Company Name] [monthly / quarterly] update. Reading time: under 3 minutes.

## TL;DR

<!-- Three bullets, one sentence each. Investor who reads nothing else grasps the state of the company. -->

- **[Top achievement]:** [One sentence with a specific number or outcome.]
- **[Primary challenge]:** [One sentence. Name it plainly.]
- **[What's next or what you need]:** [One sentence. Forward-looking.]

## Challenges

<!-- Every challenge must include what you are doing about it. A problem without a response plan signals disengagement. -->

**[Challenge 2]:** [Same structure.]

## Asks

<!-- Highest-ROI section. "Introductions to enterprise buyers" is vague. "Warm intro to [Name] at [Company], who leads [category] procurement" is actionable. -->

2. **[Hiring]:** [Role, seniority, whether you need a referral or help closing a finalist.]
3. **[Advice]:** [Specific question framed for a 2-3 sentence response.]
