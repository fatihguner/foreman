## Part 1: Events

_What happened. Stay factual. Record the moments that carried weight -- decisions, conversations, conflicts, wins, and failures. Resist the urge to interpret; that comes in Part 2._

**The most consequential decision I made this period:**

[Describe it briefly. What did I decide, and what was at stake?]

**A moment I handled well:**

[Describe a specific interaction or decision where my leadership made a positive difference. What did I do, concretely?]

**A moment I handled poorly or avoided:**

[Describe a specific interaction, decision, or conversation I mishandled or deferred. Be precise -- vague self-criticism teaches nothing.]

**Something I observed about my team that I have not yet acted on:**

[A pattern, tension, or opportunity I noticed but have not addressed.]

**An unexpected challenge and how I responded:**

[What was it, and what does my response reveal about my defaults under pressure?]

---

## Part 2: Patterns

_Step back from the events. What do they reveal about how you lead? Use the Johari Window frame: what is in your open area (known to you and others), your blind spot (known to others but not you), your hidden area (known to you but concealed), and your unknown (discovered only through honest reflection or feedback)?_

**What pattern do I see in my behavior this period?**

[e.g., "I defaulted to solving problems myself rather than coaching my direct reports to solve them." or "I avoided the difficult conversation with X for the third consecutive week."]

**Which of the four Stoic control categories consumed most of my energy?**

- [ ] Things fully within my control (my actions, my responses, my effort)
- [ ] Things partially within my influence (others' behavior, team dynamics, organizational decisions)
- [ ] Things outside my control that I worried about anyway (market conditions, competitor moves, board decisions)

**What does this distribution tell me?**

[One to two sentences.]

**Covey quadrant check -- where did my time actually go?**

- [ ] Quadrant I: Urgent + Important (crises, firefighting)
- [ ] Quadrant II: Not Urgent + Important (strategy, relationships, development)
- [ ] Quadrant III: Urgent + Not Important (interruptions, most emails, others' priorities)
- [ ] Quadrant IV: Not Urgent + Not Important (busywork, avoidance activities)

**Was I leading from Quadrant II this period, or was I pulled elsewhere?**

[One to two sentences on what pulled you off-center and why you allowed it.]

**Feedback I received (or should have sought):**

[What did others tell me -- directly or indirectly -- about my leadership? If the answer is "nothing," that itself is a signal.]

---

## Part 3: Commitments

_Not resolutions. Commitments are specific, bounded, and verifiable. Choose one from each category below. Three total._

**One thing I will START doing:**

- **What:** [Specific behavior or practice]
- **Why:** [Which pattern from Part 2 this addresses]
- **First action:** [What I will do within 48 hours to begin]
- **How I will know:** [Observable evidence that I followed through]

**One thing I will STOP doing:**

- **What:** [Specific behavior or habit to eliminate]
- **Why:** [Which pattern from Part 2 this addresses]
- **Replacement behavior:** [What I will do instead when the impulse arises]
- **Accountability:** [Who will I tell, or how will I track this?]

**One thing I will CONTINUE doing:**

- **What:** [Specific behavior that is working and deserves deliberate reinforcement]
- **Why:** [What positive outcome it produced this period]
- **How I will protect it:** [What threatens this practice, and how I will guard the space for it]

---

## Closing

_Answer one of the following. Rotate each period._

- [ ] If I were coaching someone who led exactly as I did this period, what would I tell them?
- [ ] What am I avoiding, and what is the cost of continued avoidance?
- [ ] What would the leader I aspire to become do differently than what I did this period?

[Three to five sentences. Write to your future self.]
