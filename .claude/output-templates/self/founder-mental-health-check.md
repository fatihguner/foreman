## 4. Decision Fatigue Indicators

_Every decision -- trivial or consequential -- draws from the same cognitive reservoir. Founders make hundreds daily. Track the symptoms, not the cause._

Check all that apply in the past 7 days:

- [ ] Avoided or deferred a decision I knew needed to be made
- [ ] Made a significant decision impulsively to "just get it done"
- [ ] Felt overwhelmed by choices that would normally feel manageable
- [ ] Defaulted to the easiest option rather than the best one
- [ ] Snapped at someone over something minor
- [ ] Could not prioritize -- everything felt equally urgent or equally unimportant

**Count of items checked:** [ /6]

_0-1: Normal operating range. 2-3: Elevated -- simplify your decision load this week. 4+: Acute fatigue -- delegate, cancel, or postpone everything non-essential for 48 hours._

## 5. Burnout Warning Signs

_Adapted from the Maslach Burnout Inventory's three dimensions. Burnout is not dramatic collapse -- it is gradual erosion of engagement, masked by busyness._

### Exhaustion

- [ ] Working more hours but accomplishing less
- [ ] Dreading the start of the workday
- [ ] Physical symptoms without clear medical cause (headaches, tension, stomach issues)

### Cynicism

- [ ] Feeling detached from the company's mission or customers
- [ ] Internally dismissive of team members' contributions or concerns
- [ ] Sarcasm or irritability replacing curiosity in conversations

### Reduced efficacy

- [ ] Doubting whether my work makes a difference
- [ ] Feeling like an imposter despite evidence of competence
- [ ] Losing confidence in decisions I would have made easily six months ago

**Dimensions showing signals:** [ ] Exhaustion [ ] Cynicism [ ] Reduced efficacy [ ] None

_One dimension activated: monitor closely. Two dimensions: intervene now -- adjust workload, seek support, take recovery time. Three dimensions: burnout is likely in progress. Professional support is warranted._

**Pattern check (review every 4th entry):**

- Are my composite energy scores trending down?
- Has the isolation index worsened over the past month?
- Am I checking the same burnout boxes repeatedly?
- Is the same "thing I am avoiding" appearing entry after entry?

_If the answer to two or more of these is yes, this is no longer a weekly fluctuation. Consider speaking with a coach, therapist, or trusted peer. Strength is not endurance without limit -- it is knowing when the system needs repair._
