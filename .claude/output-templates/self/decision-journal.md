# Decision Journal Entry

<!-- INSTRUCTIONS:
     Complete this entry BEFORE executing the decision, or within 24 hours of making it.
     The purpose is not to justify -- it is to create an honest record that your
     future self can learn from.
     Do not revise this entry after the outcome is known. Hindsight will corrupt
     the record. Instead, add a review addendum at the 90-day mark.
     Delete all HTML comments before archiving. -->

**Decision ID:** [YYYY-MM-DD-short-label, e.g., 2026-03-26-hire-vp-eng]

**Date of decision:** [Date]

**Review date:** [Date + 90 days]

---

## The Decision

_State it plainly. One to two sentences._

[What are you deciding to do? Be specific enough that a stranger could verify whether you followed through.]

## Context

_What is the situation that forced this decision? Capture enough detail that you will remember the circumstances in 90 days, when memory has faded and outcome bias has taken hold._

- **What triggered this decision?** [The event, deadline, or conversation that made this decision necessary now]
- **Time pressure:** [None / Low / Moderate / High / Extreme] -- [Explain]
- **Reversibility:** [Easily reversible / Reversible with cost / Partially reversible / Irreversible]
- **Stakes:** [What is at risk if this goes wrong? Be specific: money, relationships, time, reputation, opportunity cost]

## Alternatives Considered

_List every option you genuinely evaluated, not just the ones that made the final cut. Include "do nothing" if it was a real option._

| Option | Key advantage | Key disadvantage | Why rejected or selected |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **[Chosen option]** | [Primary upside] | [Primary downside] | **Selected** -- [Core reason] |
| [Alternative 1] | [Primary upside] | [Primary downside] | [Why not chosen] |
| [Alternative 2] | [Primary upside] | [Primary downside] | [Why not chosen] |
| [Do nothing / Status quo] | [What you preserve] | [What you forgo] | [Why not chosen] |

## Reasoning

_The core logic behind your choice. Write this as though explaining to a skeptical peer, not a sympathetic friend._

**The argument for this decision:**
[Two to four sentences. What is the strongest case?]

**The strongest argument against it:**
[Two to four sentences. What would a thoughtful opponent say? If you cannot articulate this clearly, you may not have considered it deeply enough.]

**What would have to be true for this decision to fail?**
[Identify the key assumptions. These are your pre-mortem indicators.]

## Confidence and Probabilities

_Calibrate your certainty. Overconfident decision-makers improve only when they track their confidence against outcomes._

- **Probability this produces the desired outcome:** [X%]
- **Confidence in my information quality:** [Low / Moderate / High]
- **Confidence in my reasoning process:** [Low / Moderate / High]

## Emotional State

_Decisions made under strong emotion deserve extra scrutiny. This section exists to flag, not to judge._

- **Dominant emotion at time of decision:** [e.g., excitement, anxiety, frustration, calm, urgency, relief]
- **Am I deciding to move toward something or away from something?** [Toward / Away]
- **Have I been influenced by any recent vivid experience?** [Yes -- describe / No]
- **Would I make the same decision if I had to explain it publicly?** [Yes / No -- why?]

## Expected Outcome

_Write this in concrete, verifiable terms. Your 90-day review will compare prediction to reality._

**If this decision succeeds, I expect the following by [date]:**
1. [Specific, observable outcome]
2. [Specific, observable outcome]

**Early indicators of success (within 30 days):**
- [Signal to watch for]

**Early indicators of failure (within 30 days):**
- [Signal to watch for]

---

## 90-Day Review Addendum

_Complete this section on or after the review date. Do not modify anything above._

**Review date:** [Actual date of review]

**What actually happened:**
[Factual outcome]

**Was the outcome better, worse, or different from expected?**
[Better / Worse / Different -- explain]

**Was the reasoning sound even if the outcome was poor (or lucky even if the reasoning was flawed)?**
[Separate process quality from outcome quality]

**What would I do differently with the same information I had then (not what I know now)?**
[This is the only question that improves future decisions]
