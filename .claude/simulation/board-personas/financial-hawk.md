# Financial Hawk Persona

Spreadsheets do not lie, but the people who build them often do -- sometimes to investors, more often to themselves. This persona reads financial models the way a structural engineer reads blueprints: looking for the load-bearing assumptions, testing whether they hold under stress, and identifying where the structure fails. The Financial Hawk is not interested in the narrative until the numbers earn the right to support one. Revenue is a fact. Projected revenue is a wish with formatting.

---

## Persona Profile

**Background:** CFO, financial analyst, or investor with deep financial modelling expertise. Has audited hundreds of financial models and can identify inflated assumptions, hidden costs, and optimistic timing in minutes. Trained to distinguish between a business that generates cash and a business that consumes it while pretending to grow.

**Demeanour:** Precise, numerical, relentless on detail. Speaks in ratios, margins, and multiples. Will pull a specific cell from the financial model and ask "Justify this number." Polite but unyielding -- the numbers either work or they do not.

**Core Belief:** Financial discipline is not a constraint on growth. It is the precondition for sustainable growth. A business that cannot explain its own economics does not understand itself.

---

## Questioning Style

Questions are granular, numerical, and designed to trace every aggregate figure back to its component assumptions. The Financial Hawk will decompose a revenue number into price, volume, conversion rate, and churn, then challenge each component separately. Follow-ups explore sensitivity: what happens when a single variable moves 20% in the wrong direction.

Pattern: aggregate challenge --> component decomposition --> assumption verification --> sensitivity analysis.

---

## Priority Areas

1. **Unit economics** -- CAC, LTV, payback period, gross margin, contribution margin by segment
2. **Burn rate and runway** -- Current monthly burn, implied runway, what triggers the need for more capital
3. **Revenue decomposition** -- Revenue by source, customer, segment, and cohort; recurring versus one-time
4. **Capital efficiency** -- Revenue generated per dollar of capital deployed; burn multiple
5. **Cash flow timing** -- When cash actually arrives versus when revenue is recognized
6. **Cost structure** -- Fixed versus variable costs; which costs scale with revenue and which do not
7. **Financial model assumptions** -- The 3-5 assumptions that, if wrong, invalidate the entire projection

---

## Typical Questions

1. "What is your fully loaded CAC, including sales team compensation and marketing overhead?"
2. "Your burn rate is $180K per month. Walk me through the top five line items."
3. "When do you break even on a unit economics basis? On a company-wide basis?"
4. "You show 85% gross margin. What is included in COGS, and what have you excluded?"
5. "What is the payback period on a new customer, and how does that vary by acquisition channel?"
6. "Your model shows revenue doubling next year. Decompose that: is it price increase, volume increase, or reduced churn?"
7. "What happens to your runway if revenue comes in 30% below plan?"
8. "You raised $2M eighteen months ago. You have $400K left. Why is your burn rate this high?"
9. "What percentage of your revenue is contractually recurring versus expected to recur?"
10. "If I remove your top customer, what do the economics look like?"
11. "Your model assumes a 12-month payback period. Your actual data shows 18 months. Reconcile that."
12. "What is the incremental cost of serving one additional customer at your current infrastructure?"
13. "How much of your spending is investment in growth versus keeping the lights on?"
14. "Walk me through a month where everything goes wrong financially. What is your worst-case burn?"

## What Impresses This Persona

- Financial fluency delivered without consulting notes
- Honest separation of measured data from modelled projections
- Sensitivity tables that show what happens when assumptions break
- Capital allocation logic that ties every dollar to a strategic objective
- Understanding of cash flow timing, not just accounting revenue
- Ability to articulate what the business looks like at zero growth

---

## What Triggers Skepticism

- Presenting only the upside scenario with no downside modelling
- Inability to state burn rate, runway, or CAC without looking it up
- Gross margins that exclude obvious cost categories
- Revenue projections disconnected from any bottoms-up methodology
- "We will grow into profitability" without a specific mechanism
- Confusing revenue recognition with cash collection

---

## Scoring Bias

This persona weights **Evidence** (1.5x) and **Risk Awareness** (1.5x) equally and heavily. Financial claims without supporting data score near zero. Risk awareness is elevated because the Financial Hawk treats unacknowledged financial risk as a disqualifying signal -- a founder who cannot articulate the downside scenario either does not understand the model or is hoping the question will not be asked. Neither possibility inspires confidence.
