# Leadership Crisis Diagnosis

## Entry Symptoms

The entrepreneur speaks in first person about themselves, not the business: "I feel like a fraud," "I'm burning out," "I can't keep up," or "I don't know what I'm doing anymore." The vulnerability in these statements is itself diagnostic — leaders in genuine crisis rarely articulate the problem cleanly, because the crisis distorts their capacity for self-assessment.

## Triage Questions

### Q1: Is this about capability or confidence?

- **Capability — "I genuinely lack the skills this role now requires"** — the role may have outgrown the leader. Proceed to Q2.
- **Confidence — "I can do it but I feel like I'll be exposed as inadequate"** — likely diagnosis: **impostor-syndrome**. Confirm with Q3.
- **Both — "I don't know if I can't do it or just think I can't"** — this ambiguity itself suggests a transition problem. Proceed to Q2.

### Q2: Has your role changed significantly in the past 6-12 months?

- **Yes — the company grew, and my job became something I didn't sign up for** — likely diagnosis: **role-transition-failure**. The founder-to-CEO transition is the most common version, but it recurs at every scaling inflection.
- **Yes — we raised funding, and now I answer to a board** — likely diagnosis: **role-transition-failure** compounded by **executive-isolation**.
- **No — my role hasn't changed, but my ability to handle it has deteriorated** — proceed to Q4. The issue is resource depletion, not role mismatch.

### Q3: When you succeed, do you attribute it to luck, timing, or other people — and when you fail, do you attribute it entirely to yourself?

- **Yes, exactly that pattern** — likely diagnosis: **impostor-syndrome**. The attribution asymmetry is the signature.
- **No — I attribute success to myself but still feel inadequate** — this is not classical impostor syndrome. The issue may be perfectionism or an actual skills gap. Return to Q2.
- **I haven't had a recent success to attribute anything to** — proceed to Q4 and Q5. The crisis may be situational rather than psychological.

### Q4: When did you last sleep well, exercise, or take a full day off?

- **Weeks or months ago** — likely diagnosis: **burnout**. Physical depletion is compounding or causing the psychological symptoms.
- **I sleep fine but wake up dreading the day** — the exhaustion is motivational, not physical. Likely diagnosis: **loss-of-purpose**. Proceed to Q6.
- **I try to rest but can't stop thinking about work** — likely diagnosis: **burnout** with anxiety features. The nervous system is stuck in threat-response mode.

### Q5: Who do you talk to honestly about how you're doing?

- **Nobody — I can't show weakness to my team, investors, or family** — likely diagnosis: **executive-isolation**. The leader is carrying the full cognitive and emotional load alone.
- **I have a coach, therapist, or peer group** — isolation is not the primary issue. Return to Q1 and Q4 for capability or depletion causes.
- **I used to have people but pushed them away as things got harder** — likely diagnosis: **executive-isolation** compounded by **burnout**. Withdrawal is both symptom and accelerant.

### Q6: Does the work still feel meaningful to you?

- **No — I've lost connection to why I started this** — likely diagnosis: **loss-of-purpose**. The mission no longer sustains the sacrifice.
- **The mission matters but I feel like the wrong person to carry it** — likely diagnosis: **impostor-syndrome** or **role-transition-failure**. The purpose is intact but the leader's relationship to it is fractured.
- **I don't know anymore — I'm too exhausted to feel anything about it** — likely diagnosis: **burnout** so severe it has suppressed the capacity for meaning-making. Address burnout first; purpose clarity will follow recovery.

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Key Signals | Confirmation Path |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Role transition failure** | Role outgrew the leader, new demands feel alien, "I didn't sign up for this" | Q2 confirms role change + Q1 shows capability doubt |
| **Impostor syndrome** | Success attributed to luck, failure internalised, persistent fear of exposure | Q3 confirms attribution asymmetry + Q1 shows confidence deficit |
| **Delegation deficit** | Leader does everything, bottleneck on all decisions, no trusted lieutenants | Q2 shows role unchanged but overwhelming + Q5 shows isolation |
| **Executive isolation** | No confidants, emotional suppression, withdrawal from relationships | Q5 confirms no support network + Q2 shows new accountability |
| **Burnout** | Sleep disruption, physical depletion, inability to disengage, dread | Q4 confirms physical symptoms + Q6 shows emotional flatness |
| **Loss of purpose** | Mission disconnection, existential questioning, "why am I doing this" | Q6 confirms meaning deficit + Q4 shows motivational exhaustion |

**Burnout:**
1. Apply `leader-resilience-and-wellbeing` — immediate stabilisation and recovery planning

1. Apply `purpose-driven-leadership` — reconstruct the connection between personal values and the venture
2. Apply `leader-self-reflection` — examine what changed and what still holds
3. Output with `leadership-reflection` and `decision-journal`

## Red Flags

- **The entrepreneur expresses suicidal ideation, self-harm, or a desire to disappear.** This is a mental health emergency. No framework, skill, or diagnostic replaces professional crisis intervention. Provide emergency resources immediately: National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (988 in the US), Crisis Text Line (text HOME to 741741), or local equivalent.
- **The leader describes substance dependence as a coping mechanism.** Addiction requires clinical treatment, not leadership coaching. Route to professional support.
- **The entrepreneur is making major irreversible decisions while in acute crisis.** Recommend a 30-day moratorium on irreversible decisions and immediate support from a therapist or executive coach.
- **Physical symptoms are present: chest pain, panic attacks, cognitive impairment, sustained insomnia.** These require medical evaluation.
