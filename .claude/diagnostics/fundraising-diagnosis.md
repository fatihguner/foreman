# Fundraising Diagnosis

Ninety percent of fundraising failures are misdiagnosed. The founder assumes the pitch deck needs better graphics. The advisor suggests more networking. The accelerator recommends "getting your story right." Meanwhile, the actual problem -- no product-market fit, no defensible unit economics, or a valuation that insults the intelligence of anyone who can operate a spreadsheet -- goes unaddressed. A polished pitch for a fundamentally unfundable business is still unfundable. This diagnostic separates the signal from the noise.

---

## Entry Symptoms

A founder presenting with fundraising difficulty typically articulates the problem in one of these forms:

- **"Investors keep saying no"** -- Repeated rejection across multiple meetings, often without clear feedback
- **"My pitch isn't working"** -- Meetings happen but never convert to term sheets
- **"VCs ghost me"** -- Initial interest evaporates; follow-up emails go unanswered
- **"Valuation pushback"** -- Investors express interest but dispute the ask
- **"I can't raise money"** -- General inability to close a round despite sustained effort

Each symptom maps to a different root cause. The triage below determines which.

---

## Triage Questions

### Level 1: Readiness

**Q1: Do you have a validated product with paying customers or measurable engagement?**
- No --> **Diagnosis: Premature Fundraising.** Most institutional investors require evidence that someone wants what you are building. Raising before validation is not bold; it is wasteful.
- Yes --> Proceed to Q2.

**Q2: Can you articulate your unit economics -- CAC, LTV, margins, payback period?**
- No / vague --> **Diagnosis: Missing Traction Proof.** Investors hear "we're growing" constantly. Without unit economics, growth is indistinguishable from spending.
- Yes --> Proceed to Q3.

### Level 2: Targeting

**Q3: Have you researched whether the investors you are pitching actually invest at your stage, in your sector, at your check size?**
- No / uncertain --> **Diagnosis: Wrong Investor Targeting.** Pitching a seed-stage SaaS company to a growth-stage biotech fund is not fundraising; it is spam.
- Yes --> Proceed to Q4.

**Q4: Are investors engaging with the substance of your business, or does the conversation stall at the pitch level?**
- Stalls at pitch level --> Proceed to Q5 (pitch assessment).
- Engages with substance but does not convert --> Proceed to Q6 (terms assessment).

### Level 3: Pitch vs. Fundamentals

**Q5: When investors pass, do they cite the market, the team, or the traction -- or do they seem disengaged from the start?**
- Disengaged from the start --> **Diagnosis: Weak Pitch / Story.** The narrative fails to create tension, stakes, or a compelling reason to act now.
- Cite specific concerns about market, team, or traction --> **Diagnosis: Missing Traction Proof** or **Premature Fundraising.** The pitch is working; the fundamentals are not.

### Level 4: Terms and Timing

**Q6: Is the pushback primarily about valuation, round size, or deal structure?**
- Valuation too high --> **Diagnosis: Unrealistic Valuation.** The gap between your self-assessment and the market's assessment is a data point, not an insult.
- "Not right now" / "Come back when..." --> **Diagnosis: Timing Mismatch.** The fundraising window, market conditions, or your milestone trajectory does not align with investor deployment cycles.
- Round size mismatch --> Revisit investor targeting (Q3).

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Core Problem | Key Indicator |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Premature Fundraising** | No product-market fit yet | No paying customers, no retention data, no usage metrics |
| **Wrong Investor Targeting** | Pitching investors mismatched to stage, sector, or size | High volume of meetings, zero follow-through |
| **Weak Pitch / Story** | Narrative fails to land | Investor attention drops within first five minutes; no emotional hook |
| **Unrealistic Valuation** | Price-to-proof ratio is inverted | Investors engage deeply but balk at terms |
| **Missing Traction Proof** | Growth claims lack supporting data | Investors ask for metrics you cannot produce |
| **Timing Mismatch** | Market cycle, fund cycle, or milestone cycle misaligned | "Interesting, but not now" repeated across multiple funds |

- **Premature Fundraising** --> `business-model-canvas` (validate the model), `unit-economics-snapshot` (build the proof)
- **Wrong Investor Targeting** --> `competitive-landscape-brief` (understand your market positioning to identify aligned investors)
- **Weak Pitch / Story** --> `storytelling-for-leaders` + `story-structure-and-arc` + `building-story-stakes` (rebuild the narrative), `fundraising-deck-outline` (restructure the deck)
- **Unrealistic Valuation** --> `unit-economics-snapshot` (ground the valuation in data), `business-model-canvas` (stress-test assumptions)
- **Missing Traction Proof** --> `unit-economics-snapshot` (build the metrics dashboard), `business-model-canvas` (identify what to measure)
- **Timing Mismatch** --> `competitive-landscape-brief` (map the market moment), `scenario-planning` (model alternative timelines)

## Red Flags

Intervene immediately if any of the following surface during triage:

- **Founder has been raising for more than 9 months without a term sheet.** The market has rendered a verdict. Continuing without fundamental change is not persistence; it is denial.
- **Founder cannot name a single comparable company's fundraising trajectory.** Insufficient market research that investors detect instantly.
- **Founder blames all rejections on investor ignorance.** Occasionally true for novel categories. Usually a defense mechanism that prevents learning.
- **Founder is raising to cover operating expenses with no growth plan.** Bridge financing without a destination -- a category investors actively avoid.
- **Personal financial desperation is driving the timeline.** Desperate founders accept terrible terms. Explore alternative funding (revenue, grants, debt) before negotiating from weakness.
