# Culture Erosion Diagnosis

Netflix famously published a 125-slide culture deck that became, in Sheryl Sandberg's words, "the most important document ever to come out of Silicon Valley." Less discussed is the fact that Netflix has revised that document repeatedly -- because culture is not a manifesto pinned to a wall. It is the aggregate of daily behaviors, reward structures, hiring decisions, and the stories people tell about what actually happens versus what the handbook claims. When founders say "our culture is breaking," they are observing a gap between the organization they intended to build and the one that now exists. The diagnostic challenge is determining where the fracture originated and whether it can be repaired -- or whether the original culture was a founding myth that never scaled.

---

## Entry Symptoms

Culture erosion surfaces through specific complaints, each pointing to a different failure:

- **"Culture is breaking"** -- A general sense that organizational norms have shifted in an undesirable direction
- **"Values feel hollow"** -- Stated values exist but no longer describe actual behavior
- **"We've lost our identity"** -- The company feels unrecognizable to long-tenured employees
- **"New hires don't fit"** -- Recent additions to the team operate by different norms
- **"The vibe has changed"** -- An atmospheric shift that employees can feel but struggle to articulate

These complaints are often dismissed as nostalgia or growing pains. They should not be.

---

## Triage Questions

### Level 1: Trigger Identification

**Q1: Did something specific trigger this change -- rapid hiring, a leadership transition, a shift to remote/hybrid work, or an acquisition/merger?**
- Rapid hiring (headcount doubled or more within 12 months) --> **Diagnosis: Hypergrowth Dilution.** Proceed to Q2 for severity.
- Leadership change (new C-suite, founder departure, new board influence) --> Proceed to Q3.
- Remote/hybrid transition --> **Diagnosis: Remote Culture Decay.** Proceed to Q4.
- Acquisition or merger --> **Diagnosis: Post-Acquisition Identity Crisis.** Proceed to Q5.
- No identifiable trigger / gradual drift --> Proceed to Q2.

### Level 2: Scope and Depth

**Q2: Is the culture problem company-wide or localized to specific teams, departments, or offices?**
- Company-wide --> The erosion is systemic. Proceed to Q3.
- Localized --> **Diagnosis: Toxic Subculture.** A specific team or leader is operating by norms that deviate from the organization's. This is a management problem, not a culture redesign problem.

### Level 3: Values Integrity

**Q3: Are the company's stated values being violated -- or were they aspirational statements that never described reality?**
- Being violated (behavior used to match values; now it does not) --> **Diagnosis: Values-Behavior Disconnect.** Something broke. The values were real but are no longer being reinforced.
- Never fully real (values were written during a branding exercise, not extracted from actual behavior) --> **Diagnosis: Leadership-Culture Gap.** The culture was never what leadership claimed. Growth simply made the gap visible.

**Q3b: Do the senior leaders personally model the stated values in their daily decisions?**
- No --> **Diagnosis: Leadership-Culture Gap.** Culture flows downhill. When leaders exempt themselves from the values they espouse, the organization learns that the values are decorative.
- Yes, but culture still eroding --> The reinforcement mechanisms (hiring, promotions, terminations, rituals) have broken. Proceed to Q4.

### Level 4: Mechanism Assessment

**Q4: Which of these cultural transmission mechanisms has failed?**

| Mechanism | Healthy Signal | Broken Signal |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Hiring** | Values-fit is weighted in interviews | "We just need bodies" / pure skills hiring |
| **Onboarding** | New hires absorb norms within 90 days | New hires operate by previous employer's norms indefinitely |
| **Promotion** | Culture carriers are promoted; culture violators are not | Highest performers promoted regardless of values alignment |
| **Storytelling** | Org legends reinforce values | No one tells founding stories; or stories contradict values |
| **Rituals** | Regular practices embody the culture | Rituals abandoned, replaced by nothing, or performed emptily |
| **Consequences** | Values violations carry real consequences | Violations tolerated for high performers or politically connected |

Multiple broken mechanisms suggest systemic erosion. A single failure is surgically repairable.

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Core Problem | Distinguishing Signal |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Hypergrowth Dilution** | New hires outnumber culture carriers; norms cannot transmit fast enough | Headcount growth outpaced onboarding and cultural integration capacity |
| **Leadership-Culture Gap** | Leaders do not embody the values they espouse | "Do as I say, not as I do" pattern visible to the organization |
| **Values-Behavior Disconnect** | Values were once real but enforcement mechanisms eroded | Long-tenured employees feel betrayed; recent hires see no problem |
| **Remote Culture Decay** | Physical distance degraded informal cultural transmission | Culture weakened specifically after remote/hybrid transition |
| **Post-Acquisition Identity Crisis** | Two organizational identities colliding without resolution | "Us vs. them" dynamics between legacy and acquired teams |
| **Toxic Subculture** | A localized group operates by deviant norms | Problem concentrated in specific teams; rest of org functions normally |

- **Values-Behavior Disconnect** --> `culture-values-document` (refresh and re-ratify values with current organization), `team-culture-and-dynamics` (rebuild enforcement rituals)
- **Remote Culture Decay** --> `team-culture-and-dynamics` (design remote-native cultural practices), `organisational-culture` (adapt transmission mechanisms for distributed work)
- **Post-Acquisition Identity Crisis** --> `kotter-change-model` (manage the cultural integration as an explicit change initiative), `organisational-culture` (forge a new shared identity)
- **Toxic Subculture** --> `team-culture-and-dynamics` (intervene at the team level), `organisational-culture` (reinforce organizational norms to contain the deviation)

## Red Flags

Address these conditions before proceeding with any cultural framework:

- **Key culture carriers are leaving.** When the people who embody the culture exit, erosion accelerates exponentially. Retaining these individuals is more urgent than any culture document.
- **The founder says "culture is fine" while employees say otherwise.** This perception gap is itself the diagnosis. Surface anonymous pulse surveys or exit interview data immediately.
- **A single toxic leader is responsible for the erosion.** No framework repairs culture while the source of toxicity holds authority. This is a personnel decision, not a culture initiative.
- **The company has never written down its values.** Oral tradition works at 10 people. At 50, it fragments. At 200, it is mythology. Codification is prerequisite, not luxury.
- **Engagement scores dropped more than 20 points in a single quarter.** This pace indicates acute crisis, not gradual erosion. Investigate for a precipitating event before applying general culture frameworks.
