# Communication Breakdown Diagnosis

Most communication failures are misdiagnosed as volume problems. The founder assumes the solution is to communicate more -- send another Slack message, schedule another all-hands, add another slide to the deck. The actual pathology is almost never insufficient quantity. It is misaligned medium, absent structure, or a fundamental inability to articulate what matters and why anyone should care.

---

## Entry Symptoms

The entrepreneur typically presents with one or more of these complaints:

- **"Nobody reads my emails."** Messages go unanswered; critical updates are missed; the founder feels invisible in their own company.
- **"The team misunderstands everything."** Instructions are followed incorrectly; deliverables miss the mark; the same clarifications repeat weekly.
- **"Investors don't get what we do."** Pitch meetings end with polite nods and no follow-up; the value proposition fails to land.
- **"Internal communication is chaos."** Information lives in too many places; no one knows the canonical source of truth; tribal knowledge dominates.
- **"Meetings are useless."** Hours consumed with no decisions made; the same topics resurface endlessly; action items evaporate.

---

## Triage Questions

Work through these sequentially. Each answer narrows the diagnosis.

1. **Medium**: Is the problem primarily in written communication (emails, documents, Slack) or verbal communication (meetings, presentations, pitches)?
2. **Audience**: Is the breakdown internal (team, co-founders) or external (investors, customers, partners)?
3. **Root type**: Is this a structural issue (no communication processes, unclear channels, missing cadence) or a skill issue (poorly written messages, weak verbal delivery, unclear thinking)?
4. **Participation**: Are the right people receiving the communication? Are the right people in the room?
5. **Frequency pattern**: Is the breakdown constant, or does it surface only at certain triggers (board meetings, product launches, conflict moments)?
6. **Feedback loop**: Has anyone told the founder directly that their communication is unclear, or is this inferred from downstream failures?

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Key Indicators | Core Problem |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Written Communication Failure** | Emails ignored, documents unread, written instructions misinterpreted | The writing lacks clarity, structure, or reader awareness; messages are too long, too vague, or too frequent |
| **Meeting Dysfunction** | No agendas, no decisions, recurring topics, wrong attendees, meetings that should have been emails | Meetings lack purpose definition, facilitation discipline, or follow-through mechanisms |
| **Audience Mismatch** | Investor pitch uses internal jargon; team updates read like board reports; customer messages feel corporate | Communication is crafted for the sender's comfort, not the receiver's context |
| **Information Overload** | Too many channels, too many updates, notification fatigue, important signals buried in noise | Volume has outpaced the organisation's capacity to filter; no hierarchy of communication importance exists |
| **Structural Gap** | No regular cadence (standups, weeklies, all-hands), no designated channels, no communication norms | The organisation lacks communication infrastructure entirely -- the problem is architectural, not personal |
| **Storytelling Deficit** | Facts delivered without narrative; data dumps without meaning; presentations that inform but fail to persuade | The founder can explain what but cannot convey why it matters -- the emotional and narrative layer is absent |

## Red Flags

- **Founder refuses feedback on their own communication.** The diagnosis may be accurate, but the intervention will fail unless the founder accepts personal responsibility rather than blaming the audience.
- **Communication problems mask deeper trust issues.** Teams that do not trust leadership will ignore even well-crafted messages. If triage reveals distrust, route to psychological-safety-practice and team-culture-and-dynamics before addressing communication mechanics.
- **Chronic meeting dysfunction paired with zero written documentation.** This combination signals organisational immaturity, not a communication skill gap. Structural intervention must precede skill development.
- **External communication failure despite strong internal communication.** The founder may lack audience translation ability -- they communicate well with insiders but cannot adapt for outsiders. Prioritise reader-psychology and storytelling skills.
- **"Nobody reads my emails" from a founder who sends 40 emails per day.** The problem is not the writing. It is the volume. Route to focus-and-priority-management first.
