# Decision Paralysis Diagnosis

Jeff Bezos distinguishes between Type 1 decisions (irreversible, consequential -- walk through a one-way door) and Type 2 decisions (reversible, low-cost -- walk through a two-way door and come back if it does not work). Most founders treat every decision as Type 1. The result is not prudence but paralysis -- weeks of deliberation over choices that could be tested in days, board meetings to resolve questions that a single phone call would answer. Worse, genuine Type 1 decisions receive the same agonized treatment, diluted by decision fatigue from all the false alarms. This diagnostic determines what is actually preventing the decision and whether the blockage is rational, emotional, structural, or self-imposed.

---

## Entry Symptoms

Decision paralysis presents with characteristic language:

- **"I can't decide"** -- The founder has identified options but cannot commit to one
- **"Should I pivot?"** -- A specific high-stakes decision with existential overtones
- **"Analysis paralysis"** -- Self-aware recognition that deliberation has replaced action
- **"Every option seems risky"** -- Risk aversion masquerading as thoroughness
- **"I keep going back and forth"** -- Oscillation between options without new information

The common mistake is treating all of these as the same problem. They are not. A founder who lacks information needs data. A founder paralyzed by fear needs a different intervention entirely.

---

## Triage Questions

### Level 1: Decision Classification

**Q1: Is this decision reversible or irreversible?**
- Reversible (can be undone within weeks/months at manageable cost) --> Flag this. Many "agonizing" decisions are actually two-way doors. If reversible, the prescription is often simply: decide now, measure, adjust.
- Irreversible or very costly to reverse --> Proceed to Q2. Deeper triage is warranted.

**Q2: How long have you been deliberating on this decision?**
- Less than one week --> Likely appropriate deliberation. Confirm the decision warrants this level of analysis and proceed to Q3.
- One to four weeks --> The cost of delay is accumulating. Proceed to Q3 with urgency.
- More than one month --> **The delay itself has become a decision** -- a decision to maintain the status quo. This must be named explicitly. Proceed to Q3.

### Level 2: Nature of the Blockage

**Q3: What specific information, if you had it, would make this decision obvious?**
- Founder can name specific missing data --> **Diagnosis: Information Deficit.** The paralysis is rational. Go get the data.
- Founder cannot identify what additional information would help --> The blockage is not informational. Proceed to Q4.

**Q4: When you imagine choosing Option A, what is the first feeling that arises? And Option B?**
- Fear, dread, anxiety dominate --> Proceed to Q5 (emotional assessment).
- Frustration or confusion dominate --> Proceed to Q6 (framing assessment).
- Different stakeholders advocate for different options --> **Diagnosis: Stakeholder Conflict.** The founder is not personally paralyzed; the organization is.

### Level 3: Emotional vs. Cognitive Blockage

**Q5: Is the fear about a specific, concrete consequence -- or is it a general sense of "what if I'm wrong"?**
- Specific consequence (e.g., "We'll run out of money," "My co-founder will leave") --> **Diagnosis: Fear of Irreversibility.** The fear is pointing at a real risk. Quantify it, mitigate it, then decide.
- General, diffuse anxiety --> **Diagnosis: Emotional Default Hijack.** The emotion, ego, social, or inertia defaults (per the Clear Thinking framework) have seized the controls. The founder knows what to do but cannot bring themselves to do it.

**Q6: Have you clearly defined what problem this decision is solving? Can you state it in one sentence?**
- No / the definition keeps shifting --> **Diagnosis: Wrong Problem Framing.** The paralysis exists because the founder is trying to answer the wrong question. Reframe first, decide second.
- Yes, clearly defined, but still stuck --> **Diagnosis: Perfectionism Trap.** The founder is waiting for a risk-free option that does not exist. Every viable path carries uncertainty, and the refusal to accept imperfection has become the obstacle.

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Core Problem | Distinguishing Signal |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Information Deficit** | Genuine missing data prevents rational choice | Founder can name the specific data that would resolve the decision |
| **Emotional Default Hijack** | Fear, ego, social pressure, or inertia has overridden judgment | Founder knows the "right" answer but cannot act on it |
| **Wrong Problem Framing** | The decision question itself is malformed | Problem definition keeps shifting; "the more I think about it, the more confused I get" |
| **Fear of Irreversibility** | A specific catastrophic outcome dominates thinking | Founder fixates on one worst-case scenario to the exclusion of all other analysis |
| **Stakeholder Conflict** | Multiple parties advocate incompatible paths | Founder oscillates based on who they spoke with last |
| **Perfectionism Trap** | Refusal to accept any option with imperfections | All options have been thoroughly analyzed; none is "good enough" |

## Red Flags

Halt triage and address these conditions directly:

- **The decision has a hard deadline within 48 hours.** Triage itself is a luxury the founder cannot afford. Skip to `tough-decision-situations` immediately.
- **The founder has made and reversed the same decision more than twice.** Oscillation erodes team confidence faster than a wrong decision does. The problem is no longer the decision itself but the founder's credibility as a decision-maker.
- **Cash runway is less than 3 months and the decision involves resource allocation.** This is a survival decision, not a strategic one. Different rules apply -- specifically, preserve optionality and minimize burn.
- **The founder is making this decision alone but it affects the entire company.** Decision paralysis in isolation often resolves when the founder involves one or two trusted advisors. Loneliness amplifies every cognitive bias.
- **Physical symptoms of stress (insomnia, inability to focus, health deterioration) accompany the paralysis.** Route to `founder-mental-health-check` before any strategic framework. A burned-out mind cannot make clear decisions regardless of the tools provided.
