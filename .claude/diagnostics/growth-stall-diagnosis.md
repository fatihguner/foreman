# Growth Stall Diagnosis

## Entry Symptoms

The entrepreneur describes a loss of momentum: "Growth has plateaued," "We hit a ceiling," or "The playbook that got us here stopped working." Revenue may still be positive, but the growth rate is decelerating — and the founder cannot pinpoint why.

## Triage Questions

### Q1: Where exactly is growth stalling?

- **User or customer acquisition** — fewer new customers per period. Proceed to Q3.
- **Revenue per customer** — customers arrive but spend less or don't expand. Proceed to Q4.
- **Team and operational capacity** — demand exists but the organisation cannot fulfil it. Proceed to Q5.

### Q2: What specifically worked before that stopped working?

- **A single acquisition channel drove most growth, and it plateaued** — likely diagnosis: **channel-exhaustion**. Confirm with Q3.
- **The product was the growth engine (virality, word of mouth), and that faded** — likely diagnosis: **product-maturity**. Confirm with Q4.
- **The founding team did everything, and that stopped scaling** — likely diagnosis: **team-scaling-failure**. Confirm with Q5.
- **Nothing specific changed — growth gradually decelerated** — likely diagnosis: **market-saturation**. Confirm with Q3 and Q6.

### Q3: Has the addressable market shifted or contracted?

- **TAM unchanged, but our share of new customers is shrinking** — a competitor is capturing what was yours. Likely diagnosis: **competitive-displacement**. Confirm with Q6.
- **Early adopters saturated and mainstream customers aren't converting** — likely diagnosis: **market-saturation**.
- **Adjacent markets exist, but we haven't entered them** — likely diagnosis: **channel-exhaustion**. The growth engine needs a new vector.

### Q4: Is the product still generating enthusiasm among customers?

- **Usage metrics declining — engagement, frequency, or NPS trending down** — likely diagnosis: **product-maturity**. The offering has run its course in current form.
- **Customers satisfied but not expanding** — the product serves a fixed need. Likely diagnosis: **market-saturation** at the account level.
- **Customers request features you haven't built** — the product needs evolution, not replacement. This is an operational bottleneck, not maturity.

### Q5: Is the team the bottleneck or the market?

- **We have demand we can't serve — hiring, onboarding, or processes are too slow** — likely diagnosis: **operational-bottleneck**. Growth is constrained by internal capacity.
- **We hired aggressively but quality and velocity dropped** — likely diagnosis: **team-scaling-failure**. The organisation outgrew its management structures.
- **The team is capable, but there's nothing more to capture in current channels** — the team is not the constraint. Return to Q3 and Q6 for market-level diagnosis.

### Q6: What has changed in the competitive landscape?

- **A well-funded competitor entered with a similar or superior product** — likely diagnosis: **competitive-displacement**. Your differentiation has eroded.
- **The category itself is commoditising — multiple players, price pressure, feature parity** — likely diagnosis: **market-saturation** compounded by commoditisation.
- **No significant competitive change** — the stall is internal. The constraint is operational, product, or channel — not market.

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Key Signals | Confirmation Path |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Market saturation** | Early adopters captured, mainstream won't convert, TAM ceiling visible | Q3 confirms segment saturation + Q6 shows commoditisation |
| **Operational bottleneck** | Demand exists, fulfilment lags, hiring or process is the constraint | Q5 confirms capacity gap + Q1 shows demand present |
| **Product maturity** | Engagement declining, NPS flat or falling, feature requests dry up | Q4 confirms enthusiasm waning + Q2 shows product was the engine |
| **Team scaling failure** | Hired fast, quality dropped, management stretched, culture diluted | Q5 confirms hiring-quality gap + Q2 shows founder-dependency |
| **Competitive displacement** | Market share shrinking, competitor gaining, differentiation lost | Q3 + Q6 confirm competitive entry with superior positioning |
| **Channel exhaustion** | Primary channel saturated, no secondary channels developed | Q2 confirms single-channel dependency + Q3 shows market still exists |

## Red Flags

- **Growth stalled because of a single customer concentration (more than 30% of revenue from one client).** This is a business risk problem, not a growth strategy problem. Address dependency before optimising for growth.
- **The company is pre-revenue and describing a "growth stall."** There is no growth to stall. Route to product-market fit validation instead.
- **The founder conflates growth rate deceleration with absolute decline.** Growing at 15% instead of 40% is a different problem from shrinking. Calibrate expectations before diagnosing pathology.
- **Regulatory or legal changes caused the stall.** Strategic frameworks cannot override compliance constraints. Engage legal counsel first.
