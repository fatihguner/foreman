# Blocker Diagnosis

Stalled implementation items are not random. They cluster around a small number of root causes, and each root cause responds to a different intervention. Treating a knowledge gap with motivational advice wastes time. Treating a fear problem with a process template wastes even more. This diagnostic separates external constraints from internal obstacles, then identifies the specific failure mode so the system can route to the right tool -- not a generic pep talk.

## Entry Symptoms

The entrepreneur says some version of: "This task has been stuck for weeks," "I keep postponing this," "I don't know how to do this step," "I'm waiting on someone," "I started but can't finish," or "This feels impossible." The item may carry `blocked` status explicitly, or it may sit in `in-progress` with zero progress delta across multiple check-ins -- the quieter and more dangerous variant.

## Triage Questions

### Q1: Is the blocker EXTERNAL (waiting on someone or something outside your control) or INTERNAL (within your control to resolve)?
- **External** -- Proceed to Q2-External.
- **Internal** -- Proceed to Q2-Internal.
- **Unclear** -- Ask: "If every external person and resource were available tomorrow, could you finish this?" A "yes" confirms external. A hesitation suggests internal.

### Q2-External: Is the external blocker a DEPENDENCY (specific person, resource, or deliverable) or an ENVIRONMENT constraint (market, regulatory, or systemic)?
- **Dependency** -- Waiting on a specific person, hire, funding, or partner. Likely diagnosis: **resource-dependency**.
- **Environment** -- Market, regulatory, or systemic constraint no individual action can accelerate. Likely diagnosis: **external-environment-block**.

### Q2-Internal: Is the internal blocker a CAPABILITY gap (don't know how), a CAPACITY gap (no time/energy), or a MOTIVATION gap (avoiding it)?
- **Capability** -- Proceed to Q3-Capability.
- **Capacity** -- Proceed to Q3-Capacity.
- **Motivation** -- Proceed to Q3-Motivation.

### Q3-Capability: Is the missing knowledge CONCEPTUAL (need to learn) or TACTICAL (know the theory, lack the how-to)?
- **Conceptual** -- Likely diagnosis: **knowledge-gap**.
- **Tactical** -- Likely diagnosis: **process-gap**.

### Q3-Capacity: Is the constraint TIME (too many priorities) or ENERGY (burnout/overwhelm)?
- **Time** -- The task keeps getting bumped. Likely diagnosis: **capacity-overload**.
- **Energy** -- Depleted across the board. Likely diagnosis: **energy-depletion**.

### Q3-Motivation: Is the avoidance RATIONAL (the task itself may be wrong) or EMOTIONAL (the task is correct but uncomfortable)?
- **Rational** -- Likely diagnosis: **wrong-task** or **motivation-misalignment**.
- **Emotional** -- Proceed to Q4-Emotional.

### Q4-Emotional: What specifically drives the avoidance?
- **Fear of failure** -- Afraid the result will expose inadequacy. Likely diagnosis: **fear-based-avoidance**.
- **Fear of success/exposure** -- Completion increases visibility or scrutiny. Likely diagnosis: **fear-based-avoidance**.
- **Perfectionism** -- Waiting for ideal conditions before acting. Likely diagnosis: **perfectionism-paralysis**.
- **Conflict avoidance** -- The task requires a difficult confrontation. Likely diagnosis: **fear-based-avoidance**.

## Diagnosis Map

| Diagnosis | Core Problem | Distinguishing Signal |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Resource dependency** | Waiting on a specific person, deliverable, or resource | Entrepreneur can name exactly who or what they are waiting for |
| **External environment block** | Market, regulatory, or systemic constraint | No individual action can accelerate resolution |
| **Knowledge gap** | Does not understand the domain well enough to execute | "I don't know where to start" |
| **Process gap** | Understands theory but lacks tactical execution method | "I get the concept but don't know the specific steps" |
| **Capacity overload** | Too many priorities competing for finite bandwidth | Task keeps losing to other items in weekly prioritisation |
| **Energy depletion** | Burnout preventing execution across the board | Progress stalled on multiple items simultaneously |
| **Fear-based avoidance** | Psychological resistance to the outcome or process | Entrepreneur hesitates, changes subject, or rationalises delay |
| **Perfectionism paralysis** | Refusal to act until conditions are ideal | All preparation complete but execution never begins |
| **Wrong task** | The task itself is flawed in conception or approach | Resistance dissolves when the task is reframed |
| **Motivation misalignment** | Task does not connect to what the entrepreneur values | "I know I should do this, but I just don't care about it" |

## Routing

**Resource dependency:** Apply `stakeholder-map` to identify escalation paths. Apply `delegation-and-empowerment` if the dependency can be reassigned. Use `blocker-analysis` template to document impact and set escalation triggers.

**External environment block:** Apply `scenario-planning` to model wait-time scenarios. Apply `stoic-control-and-clarity` to separate controllable from uncontrollable. Redirect effort to items within control.

**Knowledge gap:** Route to the specific skill that produced the original recommendation. Apply `learning-agility` to structure a learning plan. Use `learning-plan` template.

**Process gap:** Route to the relevant framework skill with emphasis on application prompts and output template sections. Apply `creative-shaping-and-craft` for strategy-to-deliverable translation.

**Capacity overload:** Apply `eisenhower-matrix` to force-rank all active items. Apply `focus-and-priority-management` to protect execution time. Apply `delegation-and-empowerment` to offload non-essential items.

**Energy depletion:** Apply `leader-resilience-and-wellbeing` to assess burnout. Apply `stoic-fortitude-and-resilience` for endurance framing. Reduce active items to three maximum until energy recovers.

**Fear-based avoidance:** Apply `emotional-courage` to name and confront the fear. Apply `stoic-fortitude-and-resilience` for courage framing. Apply `stoic-control-and-clarity` to separate feared outcomes from actual risks. Use `decision-journal` to externalise the fear.

**Perfectionism paralysis:** Apply `stoic-pragmatism` to reframe "good enough" as strategic advantage. Apply `creative-completion-and-renewal` for finishing discipline. Apply `choosing-and-executing` for the 70% rule.

**Wrong task:** Apply `defining-and-exploring-problems` to reframe what needs to happen. Apply `evaluating-and-deciding` to assess restructure or abandon. Use `decision-journal` to record the pivot rationale.

**Motivation misalignment:** Apply `wanting-what-matters` to reconnect the task to genuine priorities. Apply `purpose-driven-leadership` to test alignment. If the task truly does not matter, abandon it formally.

## Red Flags

Halt the diagnostic and redirect if any of the following conditions are present:

- **Persistent hopelessness, inability to function, or withdrawal from all activities.** This may indicate a mental health crisis. Route to professional support before applying any framework.
- **The blocker involves potential legal liability, regulatory violation, or financial fraud.** Route to qualified legal or financial counsel immediately.
- **Cash runway is under 4 weeks and the stalled item is revenue-critical.** Prioritise emergency cash management over systematic diagnosis.
- **The entrepreneur reports being in physical danger or an abusive professional relationship.** Recommend immediate professional intervention.
- **Multiple items stalled 30+ days with no engagement.** Total disengagement may signal the entrepreneur has mentally exited the venture. Surface this directly rather than treating each item individually.
